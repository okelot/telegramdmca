The problem

Telegram is one of the most stubborn channels for content theft

Pirated content on Telegram does not behave like piracy on a normal social platform. Files are passed through public channels, private groups, forwarding chains, mirrors, and bots. For content creators, that means a leak can keep resurfacing even after one post disappears. A real Telegram DMCA service needs to combine discovery, documentation, reporting, and follow-up.

Leaks spread quickly

Once a bundle or post is shared, copies can jump from one Telegram community to another before the creator even finds the original source.

Manual reporting is messy

Many victims do not know what links to save, what evidence matters, or how to structure a takedown request so it is complete and credible.

Repeat enforcement matters

The hardest part is often not the first complaint. It is identifying repost patterns, copycat channels, and recurring infringers over time.
